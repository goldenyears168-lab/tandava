# Onboarding a Teacher

**Purpose:** This guide covers everything involved in adding a new teacher to your studio in Tandava -- from sending the invitation to configuring their pay rate, assigning classes, and setting up the sub request flow. By the end, your new teacher will have a working profile, a schedule, and the tools they need to manage their classes independently.

**Who this is for:** Studio owners or administrators adding a new teacher (or substitute) to their Tandava studio.

**Estimated time:** 10--15 minutes per teacher.

---

## Step 1: Invite the Teacher

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Teachers.

1. Click the **Add Teacher** button in the top-right corner of the page.
2. Enter the teacher's **Full Name**.
3. Enter the teacher's **Email Address**. This is the email where their invitation will be sent.
4. Click **Save** to send the invitation.

The teacher will receive an email with a link to create their Tandava account (or log in if they already have one). Once they accept the invitation, they are added to your studio's staff roster.

> **Tip:** If the teacher already has a Tandava account from another studio or as a student, the invitation will link their existing account to your studio. They will not need to create a new one.

---

## Step 2: Set Their Role and Permissions

When adding a teacher (either during the initial invitation or by editing their profile afterward), assign their **role**. Tandava supports the following staff roles:

| Role | What They Can Do |
|------|-----------------|
| **Owner** | Full access to everything: settings, billing, staff management, financials, reports. Only one owner per studio (the account creator). |
| **Admin** | Same access as Owner except cannot change billing/ownership. Ideal for studio managers. |
| **Teacher** | Can view their own schedule, manage their classes (roster, check-ins), request subs, view their payroll, and see their ratings/reviews. Cannot access other teachers' data, financials, or studio settings. |
| **Front Desk** | Can manage the daily schedule, check students in, process drop-in sales, and view rosters. Cannot access payroll, financials, or studio settings. |

For most new instructors, select the **Teacher** role. If you are bringing on a studio manager who also teaches, **Admin** may be more appropriate.

> **Note:** You can change a staff member's role at any time from Studio Management > Teachers by opening their profile.

---

## Step 3: Configure Pay Rates

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Teachers > click on the teacher's card to open their profile.

Tandava supports four pay structures. Choose the one that matches your agreement with this teacher:

### Per Class

The teacher earns a flat dollar amount for each class they teach, regardless of attendance.

- **Pay Type:** Per Class
- **Pay Rate:** The dollar amount per class (e.g., $75)
- **Best for:** Studios with predictable class sizes or teachers who prefer income stability.

### Per Student (Revenue Share)

The teacher earns a percentage of the revenue generated by their class. This is calculated based on the class revenue from drop-in fees, membership allocations, and pack deductions.

- **Pay Type:** Revenue Share
- **Pay Rate:** The percentage (e.g., 40%)
- **Best for:** Studios that want to align teacher pay with class performance, or high-draw teachers who negotiate a share.

### Hourly

The teacher earns a fixed hourly rate based on the duration of their classes.

- **Pay Type:** Hourly
- **Pay Rate:** The dollar amount per hour (e.g., $50/hr)
- **Best for:** Teachers who lead varying-length sessions (workshops, privates, teacher trainings).

### Salary

The teacher earns a fixed amount per pay period regardless of how many classes they teach.

- **Pay Type:** Salary
- **Pay Rate:** The amount per pay period
- **Best for:** Full-time teachers or studio managers who also teach.

> **Tip:** The most common pay structure for yoga and movement studios is **Per Class**, typically ranging from $50--$100 depending on your market. Revenue Share (often 30--50%) is popular for experienced teachers who consistently fill their classes.

## Step 4: Set Availability and Preferred Time Slots

Tandava does not enforce a rigid availability calendar for teachers, but there are two practical ways to manage when a teacher is available:

### Option A: Communicate Directly and Schedule Accordingly

The simplest approach -- discuss availability with the teacher and then schedule their classes accordingly from Studio Management > Schedule. This is how most small to mid-size studios operate.

### Option B: Review Their Current Schedule

1. Open the teacher's profile from Studio Management > Teachers.
2. Look at the **Weekly Schedule** section at the bottom of their profile card. This shows every recurring class they are currently assigned to, including the day, time, and class name.
3. Use this view to identify open time slots before assigning new classes.

> **Tip:** When a teacher is assigned to classes at multiple locations, pay close attention to travel time. Avoid scheduling back-to-back classes at different locations without a buffer.

---

## Step 5: Assign Class Types They Can Teach

Teachers are assigned to specific classes on the schedule rather than to offering types. However, it is helpful to note which **specialties** a teacher is qualified to lead:

1. Open the teacher's profile from Studio Management > Teachers.
2. Review their **Specialties** (e.g., Vinyasa, Power Yoga, Yin, Restorative, Meditation, Ashtanga).
3. Review their **Certifications** (e.g., RYT 200, E-RYT 500, YACEP, Yin Certified).

When these fields are filled in, they serve two purposes:

- **Scheduling reference** -- when you or another admin is building the schedule, you can search and filter teachers by specialty to find the right person for a particular offering.
- **Student-facing profile** -- students can see a teacher's specialties and certifications on the public Instructors page, which builds trust and helps students choose classes.

### Scheduling a Teacher for a Class

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Schedule.

1. Click **Add Class**.
2. Select the **Offering** (e.g., Morning Vinyasa).
3. Select this teacher from the **Teacher** dropdown.
4. Choose the **Day**, **Time**, **Location**, and **Room**.
5. Save the schedule entry.

The teacher will now see this class on their personal schedule.

## Step 6: Sub Request Flow

Tandava has a built-in system for managing substitute teachers. Here is how it works from both the teacher's side and the studio admin's side.

### How a Teacher Requests a Sub

When a teacher knows they cannot make an upcoming class:

1. The teacher views their schedule and identifies the class they need covered.
2. They submit a sub request for that specific class occurrence.
3. Studio admins see the request in the **Action Items** panel on the Dashboard (e.g., "James Liu needs a sub for Wednesday 9:30 AM Gentle Flow").
4. The admin can then assign a substitute from the Schedule management page.

### How an Admin Assigns a Sub

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Schedule.

1. Find the class that needs a sub.
2. Click the three-dot menu on the class card and select **Find Sub**.
3. A dialog opens showing the class details and a dropdown of available substitute teachers. Teachers are filtered to those who have **Available for subs** enabled on their profile.
4. Select a substitute teacher from the list. The dialog shows each teacher's specialties to help you make a good match.
5. Optionally check the **Notify booked students** checkbox. When enabled, all students currently booked into that class will receive a notification about the teacher change.
6. Click **Confirm Sub**.

The schedule will update to show the substitute teacher's name, with a "Sub" badge. The original teacher's name is preserved for record-keeping.

### How a Teacher Accepts a Sub Request

When a sub request goes out and a teacher is available:

1. Teachers with **Available for subs** enabled and **Notify on sub request** enabled receive a notification.
2. The admin can directly assign them, or the teacher can indicate availability through the notification system.
3. Once confirmed by the admin, the sub appears on the substitute teacher's schedule.

### Configuring Sub Availability

1. Open the teacher's profile from Studio Management > Teachers.
2. Look for the **Available for subs** toggle at the bottom of the profile.
3. Set it to **Yes** if this teacher is willing to pick up sub shifts, or **No** if they are not.

> **Tip:** Keep "Available for subs" enabled for as many teachers as possible. Having a deep sub pool makes it much easier to cover last-minute absences. Teachers who only teach specific styles can still be available -- the admin choosing the sub will see each teacher's specialties and make an informed decision.

## Step 7: What Teachers Can See and Do (Teacher-Side View)

Once a teacher is onboarded, here is what they have access to in Tandava:

### Their Schedule

Teachers can see their upcoming classes, including:

- Class name, day, and time
- Room and location
- Number of students booked and capacity

### Class Rosters

For each class they teach, teachers can view the student roster, see who has checked in, and check students in themselves.

### Sub Requests

Teachers can request a sub for any of their upcoming classes. The request is sent to studio admins for fulfillment.

### Sub Opportunities

If they have sub availability turned on, they will be notified when another teacher needs a sub for a class that matches their skills.

### Payroll

Teachers can view their own payroll summary:

- Number of classes taught this month
- Earnings this month
- Pay rate and type

### Profile

Teachers can view and update their own profile, including contact information, specialties, certifications, and bio.

> **Note:** Teachers cannot see other teachers' pay rates, earnings, or personal details. They also cannot access studio financials, student personal data beyond class rosters, or studio settings.

---

## Step 8: Payroll Tracking

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Reports (Payroll Summary section) or Studio Management > Financials.

Tandava automatically tracks payroll based on each teacher's configured pay type and the classes they teach:

### How Pay Is Calculated

- **Per Class:** Number of classes taught multiplied by the per-class rate.
- **Revenue Share:** Class revenue (from drop-ins, membership allocations, and pack deductions) multiplied by the teacher's share percentage.
- **Hourly:** Class duration multiplied by the hourly rate.
- **Salary:** Fixed amount per pay period.

### Viewing Payroll

1. Go to Studio Management > Reports.
2. Scroll to the **Payroll Summary** section.
3. You will see:
   - **Total Payroll This Month** -- the combined amount owed to all teachers.
   - A per-teacher breakdown showing: teacher name, number of classes, calculated earnings, and pay type.
4. Click **View Details** for a full payroll breakdown with individual class-level entries.

### Exporting Payroll

From the Reports page, click **Export** to download payroll data as a CSV file. This is useful for feeding into your payroll provider or accounting software.

> **Tip:** Review payroll at the end of each pay period before processing payments. Check for any sub assignments that may have affected pay (for example, if a sub was paid per-class for a class they covered, that should appear in their payroll rather than the original teacher's).

## Quick Reference: Teacher Profile Fields

| Field | Where It Appears | Required |
|-------|-----------------|----------|
| Full Name | Teacher cards, schedule, student-facing profile | Yes |
| Email | Invitation delivery, contact | Yes |
| Phone | Teacher profile (admin view) | No |
| Role | Determines permissions | Yes |
| Specialties | Teacher cards, search filter, public profile | Recommended |
| Certifications | Teacher profile, public profile | Recommended |
| Pay Type | Payroll calculation | Yes |
| Pay Rate | Payroll calculation | Yes |
| Available for Subs | Sub request system | Defaults to Yes |
| Active/Inactive | Controls schedule visibility | Defaults to Active |
