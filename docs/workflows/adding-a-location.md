# Adding a Location

**Purpose:** This guide explains how to add a new physical location to an existing studio in Tandava. Whether you are expanding to a second space across town or opening a dedicated workshop room in a neighboring building, this workflow covers everything from initial setup to scheduling classes at the new location.

**Who this is for:** Studio owners or administrators who already have a studio set up in Tandava and need to add an additional location.

**Estimated time:** 15--20 minutes.

---

## When to Add a Location vs. Create a New Studio

Before you begin, decide whether you need a new **location** or an entirely separate **studio**.

**Add a location** when:

- The new space operates under the same studio brand and business entity
- You want a single dashboard, unified student roster, and shared teacher pool
- Students purchase one membership or class pack that works across all your spaces
- Financial reporting and payroll should be consolidated

**Create a separate studio** when:

- The new space is a distinct brand with its own name, website, and identity
- You want completely separate student lists, pricing, and financials
- The spaces are operated by different business entities or ownership groups

> **Tip:** Most studio owners expanding to a second or third space within the same city will want to add a location. If you are unsure, start with a location -- you can always split it into a separate studio later.

---

## Step 1: Open the Locations Settings

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Settings > Locations tab.

You will see your existing location(s) listed here with their address, rooms, and an Edit button. At the top of the section, click **Add Location**.

---

## Step 2: Set Up the New Location

Fill in the following details for your new space:

1. **Location Name** -- A short, recognizable name that distinguishes this location from your others (e.g., "Mission District Studio" or "North Beach Space"). This name appears in the schedule, booking flow, and teacher assignments.

2. **Street Address** -- The full street address including city, state, and zip code. This is displayed to students in the schedule and booking confirmation.

3. **Rooms** -- List the rooms or practice spaces at this location as a comma-separated list (e.g., "Studio A, Studio B, Private Room"). Each room can be independently scheduled, and room capacity can differ.

4. **Amenities** -- Note any amenities available at this location (e.g., "Parking, Showers, Mat Storage, Retail Area"). These help students know what to expect.

5. **Phone** -- An optional direct phone number for this location, if it differs from your main studio contact number.

6. Click **Save** to create the location.

> **Note:** The first location you created during onboarding is automatically flagged as your **primary location**. The primary location is the default shown in the schedule view and student-facing pages. You can change which location is primary from the Locations settings at any time.

---

## Step 3: Configure Location-Specific Settings

Some settings in Tandava can vary by location. After creating the location, review the following:

### Room Capacity

Each room at a location can have its own capacity. When you create a schedule entry and assign it to a specific room, the class capacity defaults to the offering's configured capacity -- but you can override it per room if one space is larger or smaller than another.

### Location Visibility

By default, new locations are marked as **active** and visible to students. If you are not ready to show this location publicly yet (for example, the space is still being built out), you can set it to inactive until you are ready to go live.

### Policies

Cancellation windows, late-cancel fees, no-show fees, and waitlist settings are configured at the **studio level** (Settings > Policies) and apply to all locations. If you need to communicate location-specific rules, include them in the class description or a note on the offering.

---

## Step 4: Assign Existing Offerings to the New Location

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Offerings.

Your existing offerings (class types) are defined at the studio level and are not tied to a specific location. This means any offering can be scheduled at any location. There is no special "assignment" step -- you simply schedule the offering at the new location when you build the schedule (next step).

However, this is a good moment to consider whether the new location needs any new offerings:

1. Does the new space support class types that your original location does not? For example, if the new location has a hot room and your original does not, you may want to create a "Hot Vinyasa" offering.
2. Does the new location have smaller rooms that need lower-capacity versions of existing classes? You can either adjust the capacity on individual schedule entries or create a dedicated offering variant.
3. Click **New Offering** to create any new class types specific to the new location's capabilities.

> **Tip:** Keep your offering list clean. If you teach "Morning Vinyasa" at both locations with the same format and pricing, you do not need to create a separate offering for each location. The location is determined by the schedule entry, not the offering itself.

---

## Step 5: Build the Schedule for the New Location

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Schedule.

1. Click **Add Class**.
2. Select the **Offering** (class type).
3. Select the **Teacher** who will lead this class.
4. Choose the **Day of the Week**.
5. Set the **Start Time**.
6. Select the **Location** -- choose your newly created location from the dropdown.
7. Select the **Room** within that location.
8. Save the schedule entry.
9. Repeat for each class at the new location.

The schedule view shows the location and room for each class, so you and your staff can easily distinguish between locations at a glance.

> **Tip:** When viewing the schedule, you can filter by location to see only the classes at a specific space. This is especially helpful once you have a full schedule across multiple locations.

---

## Step 6: Staff Assignments for Multi-Location Studios

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Teachers.

Teachers in Tandava are assigned at the studio level and can teach at any location. There is no need to "assign" a teacher to a location separately -- they are assigned to specific classes on the schedule, and each class belongs to a location.

However, there are a few things to keep in mind:

### Review Teacher Availability

Before scheduling a teacher at the new location, confirm they are available and willing to travel to that space. Check their existing schedule to avoid conflicts:

1. Click on a teacher's card to open their profile.
2. Review their **Weekly Schedule** section to see their current commitments.
3. Make sure there is enough travel time between locations if they teach at both.

### Hiring for the New Location

If you need to bring on new teachers specifically for the new location, see the [Onboarding a Teacher](./onboarding-a-teacher.md) workflow. New teachers are added to your studio's teacher roster and can then be scheduled at any location.

### Sub Pool

Teachers who have **Available for subs** enabled in their profile can be called on to cover classes at any location. When requesting a sub (from Schedule > class actions > Find Sub), you will see all available substitute teachers regardless of location.

> **Note:** If a teacher only wants to sub at certain locations, coordinate this through direct communication. Tandava's sub system shows all available teachers studio-wide.

---

## Step 7: Update Your Student-Facing Experience

Once the new location is set up and classes are scheduled, students will see the new location in several places:

- **Schedule view** -- classes at the new location appear alongside classes at your other location(s), with the location name and room displayed on each entry.
- **Booking flow** -- when a student books a class, the confirmation shows the location address so they know where to go.
- **Studio detail page** -- if your studio is listed on the Discovery Directory, the new location will appear on your public studio page.

> **Tip:** Consider sending a notification or email to your existing student base announcing the new location. You can use the Notify Students feature from the Schedule page to reach students who are booked into upcoming classes, or send a broader announcement through your regular email tool.

## Tips for Managing Multi-Location Studios

### Use Clear Location Names

Choose names that are immediately recognizable. "SOMA" and "Mission" are better than "Location 1" and "Location 2." Students will see these names throughout the schedule and booking experience.

### Monitor Attendance by Location

**Navigate to:** Studio Management > Reports.

The Reports page shows attendance metrics, top classes by attendance, and teacher performance. Use these reports to compare how classes perform at each location. If a class fills up consistently at one location but not the other, consider adjusting the schedule.

### Consistent Pricing Across Locations

Memberships and class packs in Tandava work across all locations by default. A student with an Unlimited Monthly membership can attend classes at any of your locations. If you need to restrict a membership or pack to specific locations, you can configure that when creating or editing the membership type or class pack type in Financials.

### Coordinate Teacher Schedules Carefully

The most common challenge with multi-location studios is scheduling conflicts. A teacher cannot be in two places at once. When building the schedule, always check for overlapping time slots across locations. The Schedule management view shows all classes for all locations on a given day, which helps you spot conflicts.

### Plan for Location-Specific Equipment Needs

If your locations have different equipment (e.g., one has aerial silks, another has reformers), create offerings that reflect those differences. Students will naturally see only the classes available at each location when browsing the schedule.
