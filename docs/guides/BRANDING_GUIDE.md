# Studio Branding Guide

How to make Tandava feel like *your* studio—confidently and without worry.

---

## The 2-Minute Brand Setup

Most studios only need this:

1. **Upload your logo** — It appears in the corner of your admin and on all customer pages
2. **Pick your primary brand color** — Buttons, links, and accents adapt automatically
3. **Done** — Everything else adjusts to match

That's it. Your studio now feels like yours.

Want more control? Keep reading. But know that you don't *have* to do anything else.

---

## Why Branding Works Differently Here

Tandava has two kinds of screens, and they serve different purposes.

**Your staff tools** (scheduling, rosters, reports) are designed to be fast and familiar. When a new instructor joins, they should recognize where things are immediately. These screens prioritize clarity over creativity—and that's intentional.

**Your customer-facing pages** (your website, booking pages, event listings) are where your brand should shine. These are the first impression. They're designed to be flexible, expressive, and uniquely yours.

This separation helps everyone:
- Staff stay efficient without relearning the interface
- Customers experience your brand, not generic software
- You get creative freedom where it matters most

---

## The Two Places Your Brand Shows Up

### A. Internal App (Staff-Facing)

This is where your team works—schedules, check-ins, member management, reports.

**What branding looks like here:**
- Your logo in the navigation
- Your primary color as an accent
- That's intentionally it

**Why the restraint?**
Your staff will use this daily. Consistency reduces mistakes and training time. When everything looks and works the same way, new hires get up to speed faster and experienced staff stay efficient.

Think of it like a professional kitchen—functional, organized, predictable. The creativity happens in what you serve, not in rearranging the stove.

### B. Consumer-Facing Pages

This includes your:
- Studio website and landing pages
- Class schedule and booking flow
- Event and workshop promotions
- Pricing and membership pages
- Member portal (where clients manage their account)

**Why these are different:**
These pages are your storefront. They should feel like walking into your physical studio—your colors, your voice, your vibe. The system is designed to let you express that freely.

### Quick Comparison

| Aspect | Staff Tools | Customer Pages |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Purpose** | Operations | Brand & conversion |
| **Customization** | Light (logo, accent color) | Full (colors, fonts, layouts, imagery) |
| **Priority** | Speed and clarity | Emotion and expression |
| **Changes affect** | Staff workflows | Customer experience |

## What You Can Customize

These are yours to own. Go ahead.

### Brand Colors
- **Primary color** — Your main brand color (buttons, links, highlights)
- **Secondary color** — Supporting accent for variety
- **Background tones** — Light/dark preferences for your pages

### Typography
- **Display font** — For headlines and impact
- **Body font** — For readable text (we recommend keeping this clean)

### Logo
- Upload versions for light and dark backgrounds
- The system places it appropriately everywhere

### Imagery
- Hero images on landing pages
- Background textures or patterns
- Instructor and studio photography
- Class and event imagery

### Page Layouts (Customer-Facing)
- Choose from layout options for your homepage
- Arrange sections (schedule, instructors, testimonials, pricing)
- Feature different content for different audiences

### Tone and Content
- Headlines and taglines
- Call-to-action text
- How you describe your classes and offerings
- The personality in your words

This is where your studio becomes unmistakably *yours*.

## What Stays Consistent (And Why That's Good)

Some things don't change, and that's a feature:

**In the staff tools:**
- Table layouts and data presentation
- Form spacing and input styles
- Navigation structure
- Button sizes and placements
- Modal and dialog behavior

**Why?**
- Staff build muscle memory—they know where things are
- New team members orient quickly
- Errors decrease when interfaces are predictable
- You don't have to retrain anyone when you update your brand colors

Think of it as a gift to your future self and your team. The system handles the hard parts so you can focus on running your studio.

## Levels of Customization

Not everyone wants the same depth. Here's how to think about it:

| Level | Who It's For | What's Involved |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Basic** | Most studios | Logo + primary color. Done in 2 minutes. |
| **Moderate** | Design-curious owners | Fonts, imagery, section layouts. An afternoon project. |
| **Full** | Working with a designer | Custom page designs, event themes, photography direction. |

All three are valid. Start where you're comfortable.

## If You're Working With a Designer or Agency

External designers can do great work—if they understand the boundaries.

**Share with them:**
- This guide (so they understand the two surfaces)
- Your brand assets (logo files, color codes, fonts)
- Examples of studios or brands you admire
- The specific pages you want customized

**Language to use:**
- "We're customizing *customer-facing pages only*"
- "The admin interface stays as-is—that's by design"
- "Focus on the booking flow, landing pages, and event pages"

**Avoid early:**
- Requests to "redesign the dashboard"
- Mockups that change how staff tools work
- Suggestions that require "just a small code change"

If a designer pushes back on these boundaries, share the "Why Branding Works Differently" section above. Good designers will appreciate the clarity.

## If You're Using AI or a No-Code Tool

AI design tools can help you generate:
- Landing page layouts
- Color palette suggestions
- Image treatments
- Copy variations

**Guardrails to keep in mind:**
- Generate ideas for *customer-facing pages only*
- If a suggestion involves changing navigation or data displays, skip it
- Simpler is usually better—complexity often hurts more than it helps

**Signs something might be "off":**
- It looks dramatically different from other modern studio sites
- Text is hard to read or buttons are hard to tap
- It requires explanation to understand
- Your gut says "this feels busy"

Trust your instincts. If it feels complicated, it probably is.

## Your Member Portal

A quick note on the member portal—where your clients log in to see their bookings, buy class packs, or manage their membership.

This follows your *customer* brand, not your staff tools. It's their experience of your studio, so it should feel welcoming and on-brand.

When you customize colors and fonts for customer-facing pages, the member portal updates too.

## Before You Publish: A Quick Checklist

Run through this before making brand changes live:

- [ ] **Is this a customer-facing page?** (If it's staff-facing, keep it simple)
- [ ] **Can I still read everything easily?** (Contrast matters)
- [ ] **Do buttons look tappable on a phone?** (Test on mobile)
- [ ] **Would a first-time visitor understand where to click?**
- [ ] **Would a new instructor still feel oriented?** (For any staff-visible changes)
- [ ] **Does this feel like my studio?** (Trust your gut)

If you check all boxes, you're good to go.

## If You Really Want to Customize Staff Tools

Some studios have unique operational needs. If you find yourself wanting to change how the admin interface works—not just how it looks—that's a different conversation.

These requests are best handled through:
- **Feature requests** — If many studios need it, we may build it
- **Developer customization** — For truly unique workflows

We keep the core staff experience consistent because it genuinely helps your team. But we're always listening to what studios need.

## A Final Word

Branding isn't about perfection. It's about expression.

Your studio has a personality—a feeling people get when they walk through your door. Your digital presence should echo that. Not replicate it exactly, but *echo* it.

The system is designed to make that easy:
- Guardrails exist so you can experiment without worry
- Defaults are sensible so you're never starting from zero
- Flexibility is built in so you can grow into it

Make Tandava feel like yours. That's what it's for.
