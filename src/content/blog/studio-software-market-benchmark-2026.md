If you run a studio, you've probably wondered what everyone *else* is booking on —
and whether the platform you're on is still the sensible default. So we went and
looked: not at stale directory listings, but at the actual live Book/Schedule
link of **301 independent studios** across 32 cities and 9 countries. This is the
competitive picture as best we can see it.

> **First, the honest caveats.** This is *our own research and opinion*, not vendor-supplied or independently audited data. It's **directional, not definitive**: it reflects a curated sample of **301 studios across 32 cities and 9 countries**, which skews toward studios with a findable, English-language web presence — so read it as "where the wind is blowing," not a census. We verified each studio's *current* platform by following its live Book/Schedule link in June 2026, but software, pricing, and features change constantly. **Always confirm the current details on each vendor's own site before making a decision.** Company and product names are trademarks of their respective owners; we are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or speaking for any of them. None of this is legal, financial, or business advice.

## What the field runs on now

No single platform owns this market anymore. Mindbody is still the most common
choice, but it's one bloc among several — and nearly a quarter of studios skip
dedicated booking software entirely, taking reservations through their own
website.

## The trend worth watching: the field is drifting off legacy

**1. Newer studios start on newer software.** Among the studios where we could
confirm a founding year, the generation mix shifts steadily by era. Post-2020
studios are the first cohort where modern platforms out-adopt legacy outright.

## Platform norms depend entirely on your market

Here's the caveat that matters most before you act on any of the above: the
"norm" is regional. Software adoption in North America looks nothing like a
destination market in Asia-Pacific or Latin America.

If you operate in North America, booking software is the baseline and the real
question is *which one*. If you operate in a destination market — Bali, Thailand,
Sri Lanka, Mexico, Central America — the norm is WhatsApp, Instagram, your own
site, or simply walking in. There, adopting a full platform makes you the
outlier: possibly a differentiator, possibly friction your clientele doesn't
actually want.

## Branded apps come included now

**98 studios (32%)** offer a branded or own-name app. Almost all are
platform-provided white-labels (Mindbody, Momence, Mariana Tek, Walla), not custom
builds. The takeaway: a branded app is no longer a development project — it ships
with the modern platforms.

## If you take three things from this

1. **The independent field is migrating off Mindbody and WellnessLiving** toward
   Momence, Walla, Arketa, and Mariana Tek — and new studios increasingly *start*
   there. Factor that direction into any platform decision, then verify the
   specifics yourself.
2. **Software norms are regional.** Match your market; don't import another
   region's playbook.
3. **A branded app is table stakes** on modern platforms, not a reason on its own
   to switch.

Whatever you choose, the goal isn't to follow the herd — it's to find the booking
experience that fits *your* studio, your members, and your market. Every platform
here genuinely serves some studios beautifully. The art is matching the tool to
the room.
