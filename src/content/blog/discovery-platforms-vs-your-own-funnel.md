There are really two kinds of studio software, and they answer two different
questions. A **discovery platform** (Mindbody, ClassPass) answers *"how do new
people find me?"* A **white-label app** (Arketa, Glofox, Walla, and most others)
answers *"how do I serve the people I already reach?"* They look similar on a
feature grid and are completely different businesses to run. Knowing which one
you're actually buying is, in our opinion, the most consequential software
decision a studio makes.

This builds on the economics in our
[pricing piece](/blog/studio-software-pricing-and-what-the-cut-buys); here we
focus on the marketing model behind each.

## Two models, side by side

| | Discovery platform (marketplace) | White-label app / your own funnel |
|---|---|---|
| Examples | Mindbody marketplace, ClassPass | Arketa, Glofox, Walla, Vagaro, Momence, Tandava |
| Who finds the member | The platform's consumer app/network | You — social, ads, referrals, website, walk-ins |
| What you pay for | Discovery (a new client) | Software and your own marketing |
| Typical cost shape | A cut of new-client revenue | Flat/subscription fee; ad + content spend is on you |
| Your brand | Shares shelf space with competitors | Front and center; it's your listing |
| Best when | You need *more new people* | You already have demand to serve and retain |

Most studios end up using a **combination**: a marketplace presence for
top-of-funnel reach plus their own branded experience for everything after. The
mistake is paying marketplace prices while doing all your own marketing — or
running a white-label app and wondering why no new faces appear.

## The case for a marketplace

A consumer marketplace does the genuinely hard, expensive thing: it puts your
schedule in front of people who have never heard of you. Mindbody's network and
ClassPass's roaming-membership model are both, at heart, **demand aggregators**.
For a new studio, an underfilled off-peak schedule, or a market where you have no
brand yet, that reach is real and valuable.

The fair-trade version: you pay a cut **only when the platform actually sources a
new client**. Mindbody's marketplace commission is 20% of the first purchase,
capped at $30, and $0 on clients you already had or sourced yourself. If that
person becomes a high-value member, that's a bargain compared to the ~$63–300 it
typically costs a studio to acquire a member through ads.
([Mindbody marketing fees](https://support.mindbodyonline.com/s/article/217038307-What-are-Marketing-Platform-fees),
[WordStream 2025 benchmarks](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/2025-google-ads-benchmarks))

ClassPass is a different flavor of the same idea: it fills otherwise-empty spots
with its members and pays the studio per attendance. The trade-off studios weigh
is rate and brand control — the upside is incremental fills you wouldn't have
had. (ClassPass terms are negotiated and largely non-public; **confirm specifics
directly** rather than relying on generalizations.)

## The case for your own funnel

A white-label app flips the model: the app is **yours**, your members download
*your* brand, and there's no marketplace cut because there's no marketplace. The
catch is that discovery is now entirely your job. The app is the destination; you
still have to build the road to it with:

- **Social and content** — the organic top of your funnel.
- **Paid acquisition** — Google/Meta ads against your lead lists and lookalikes.
- **Referrals and community** — usually the highest-ROI channel a studio has.
- **Your website and local SEO** — the owned asset that compounds over time.

This is the right model when you already generate demand and want to **own the
relationship and the margin** — no percentage skimmed off members you brought in
yourself. It's more marketing work, but the upside compounds, and your customer
list is yours.

## How to tell which one you need

Ask one question first: **where do your members come from today?**

- **Mostly from you** (Instagram, referrals, website, walk-ins) → you're buying a
  great booking-and-retention engine. A white-label app or a clean, no-cut
  platform fits, and a marketplace cut on clients you already own is mostly a
  tax.
- **You need more new people** → a marketplace's discovery is worth paying for,
  *on the clients it genuinely sources*. Use it for top-of-funnel and migrate
  loyal members into your own lower-cost channels over time.
- **Both** (the common answer) → run a hybrid deliberately: marketplace for
  reach, your own app/site/list for retention and big-ticket sales, and keep an
  eye on which channel actually sources each new member so you know what you're
  paying for.

## Where Tandava sits (full disclosure)

We build [Tandava](https://github.com/TaylorONeal/tandava), an open-source,
self-hosted studio platform — so we're firmly in the "your own funnel" camp by
design: no marketplace, no cut, your data and brand. That's a genuine bias, and
it's also a genuine limitation. We have **no discovery network** to offer, and
Tandava is an early V1 alpha, not a drop-in replacement for the platforms above.
If what you need most right now is *new people*, a marketplace will do something
we simply can't. We'd rather tell you that than pretend otherwise.

The empowering truth underneath all of this: there's no universally "right"
answer, only the right fit for where your studio is today. Every platform here
helps real studios thrive. The goal isn't to win a comparison — it's to know
exactly what you're paying for, so the technology works for you instead of the
other way around.
