Which studio platform is "best"? Wrong question. Each of these tools is genuinely
strong somewhere, and the right one depends entirely on the studio you run. What
follows is our read on how *deep and mature* sixteen capabilities are across
eight platforms — an opinion meant to help you shortlist, not a verdict.

If you haven't yet, start with
[how to choose studio software](/blog/how-to-choose-studio-software) for the
framing. This post is the detail.

## Read this first: it's our best guess, not a verdict

Three things are true at once in this category, and they shape every rating
below:

- **Features move fast** — a "no" today can be a "yes" next quarter, and
  marketing pages often run ahead of what's actually live.
- **Tiering is murky** — the same feature can be free on one product and a
  pricey add-on on another, and you often can't confirm what's "included"
  without a sales call.
- **More features is not the scorecard** — a feature built for a 40-location
  franchise is the wrong shape for a single-room studio, and the reverse.

So every rating here is **an opinion about depth and maturity**, nothing more.
Add-on-gated or shallow implementations are scored lower on purpose. If you run
one of these platforms and we got something wrong, please send the correction.

## The one-paragraph read on each

**Mindbody — the incumbent.** Twenty-plus years of depth in scheduling,
contracts, POS, and enterprise multi-location. Its real edge is the large
consumer marketplace, not the feature list. Marketing, leads, and advanced
analytics are real but gated to the upper tiers.
([mindbodyonline.com](https://www.mindbodyonline.com/business/pricing))

**Arketa — modern, wellness-first.** Best-in-class white-label app (iOS,
Android, Apple TV, Roku) and genuinely strong video, events, and retreats in the
base tier. Weak spot is integrations: many connectors and Zapier, but no
documented open public API. ([arketa.com](https://www.arketa.com/pricing))

**Momence — video- and LMS-heavy.** Flagship strength is native on-demand video
and courses, plus a top-rated consumer app. Reporting is the consistent soft
spot, and website tooling is embed-only. Now part of Xplor Technologies after
acquisition. ([momence.com](https://momence.com/pricing))

**WellnessLiving — the "Mindbody alternative."** The most complete on paper, and
it backs it on reporting, forms, widgets, and multi-location. The honest gap is
video: livestream (FitLIVE) and on-demand (FitVID) are separate paid add-ons.
([wellnessliving.com](https://www.wellnessliving.com/pricing/))

**Vagaro — broad and transparent.** The horizontal generalist with the
strongest retail/POS and a true website builder at this price. CRM and lead
pipeline are the shallow part. Most transparent pricing in the set.
([vagaro.com](https://www.vagaro.com/pro/pricing))

**Glofox (ABC Glofox) — app-first, franchise-minded.** Strong on the branded app
and multi-location, which is the ABC Fitness DNA. Trade-offs: shallow video, no
real website builder, and no standalone AI suite yet. Most pricing is
quote-gated. ([glofox.com](https://www.glofox.com/plans/))

**Walla — boutique-studio favorite.** Strong-to-full across the board, with a
clean-UX reputation, real retention AI (WallaPredict), and tidy payment
economics. Integration breadth is narrower than Vagaro's but modern. Founded
2020 by ex-Mindbody people; independent as of June 2026.
([hellowalla.com](https://www.hellowalla.com/us/pricing))

**Tandava — open source, V1 alpha (that's us).** Our project, and the honest
outlier. Genuinely working today: scheduling, memberships and class packs,
staff/payroll, waivers with digital signatures, multi-location, an embeddable
booking widget, a CSV importer that reads Mindbody/Momence/Walla exports, and
workshop checkout with tiered and deposit pricing. **Not built yet:** retail POS,
branded/white-label apps, on-demand video, and AI. It is a single-maintainer V1
alpha (AGPL-3.0) and **not a drop-in replacement for any platform here** — we
include it for honesty, not parity.
([github.com/TaylorONeal/tandava](https://github.com/TaylorONeal/tandava))

## A quick ownership note

One conflation worth clearing up: **Glofox and Walla are not the same company and
did not combine.** Glofox is owned by ABC Fitness Solutions (acquired August
2022); Walla is an independent, VC-backed competitor that raised a strategic
round in October 2025. As of June 2026 there's no public deal putting Walla under
ABC or anyone else.
([ABC/Glofox](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/abc-fitness-solutions-completes-its-acquisition-of-glofox-paving-the-way-to-create-one-of-the-largest-global-fitness-tech-companies-301612814.html),
[Walla raise](https://athletechnews.com/walla-5-million-strategic-investment-ai-powered-fitness-studio-performance-software/))

## The honest summary

**WellnessLiving and Walla** look the most complete across the board, for
different buyers: WellnessLiving leans deep, reporting-heavy, and multi-location;
Walla is the boutique favorite with the cleanest UX and strongest retention AI.
**Mindbody** stays the depth incumbent, though much of its strength lives in the
upper tiers and in its marketplace. **Vagaro** is the transparent generalist with
real retail and a true website builder. **Arketa and Momence** are the modern,
video-and-events-forward challengers yoga studios keep shortlisting. **Glofox**
is app-first and franchise-minded. And **Tandava** is the open, honest
outlier — useful, evolving, and deliberately not pretending to be any of them.

Feature depth and price are two different questions, and conflating them is how
studios end up overpaying for boxes they'll never check. The money side —
transaction cuts, app-store mechanics, and what a platform fee is actually worth
— is its [own post](/blog/studio-software-pricing-and-what-the-cut-buys).
