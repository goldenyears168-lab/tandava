The best studio software is not the one with the most features. It is the one
that fits how your studio actually works — how members find you, how you run
classes and workshops, how you get paid, and how much time you have to manage
the thing. Start there, and the choice gets much simpler.

This guide is the front door to a short series comparing the major platforms —
Mindbody, Arketa, Momence, WellnessLiving, Vagaro, Glofox, Walla — and our own
open-source project, Tandava. The honest headline up front: **every one of these
tools is genuinely good at something, and real studios thrive on each of them.**
Software is leverage. Used well, it gives you back hours, smooths the member
experience, and lets you spend more of your energy actually serving students.
That is the whole point — technology as empowerment, not as a tax on your time.

So this is not a "winner." It is a way to find *your* fit.

## Three things that make this genuinely hard

Before any comparison, three truths are worth naming, because they explain why
shopping for studio software feels so murky:

1. **Features move fast.** Every platform here ships changes monthly. A "no"
   today can be a "yes" next quarter, and marketing pages often run ahead of
   what is actually live.
2. **Tiering is murky.** The same capability can be base-tier on one product and
   a $200 add-on on another. Whether something is "included" is frequently
   impossible to confirm without a demo or a real implementation.
3. **More features is not the scorecard.** A tool can check every box and still
   be the wrong shape for you. Software built for a 40-location franchise is
   usually wrong for a single-room studio — and the reverse is just as true.

Hold those in mind and you will read every comparison (including ours) with the
right amount of salt.

## Start from your needs, not the feature grid

A handful of questions will narrow the field faster than any spec sheet:

- **Where do your members come from?** If most discovery happens through your
  own Instagram, referrals, and website, you are buying a great booking and
  retention engine. If you want a platform to *bring you new clients*, that is a
  different (and more valuable) thing — and it is the subject of
  [a later post in this series](/blog/discovery-platforms-vs-your-own-funnel).
- **What's your studio's shape?** Single boutique studio, multi-location, or a
  franchise? This drives both feature fit and how pricing scales.
- **Do you run big-ticket programs?** Teacher trainings, retreats, and workshops
  are large transactions with deposits and payment plans, and they change the
  math considerably (more in our [pricing piece](/blog/studio-software-pricing-and-what-the-cut-buys)).
- **Do you sell digital content?** On-demand video and livestream are where
  platforms differ most — and where app-store rules quietly bite.
- **How do you want to pay for it?** Flat monthly fee, per location, per
  bookable calendar, or a percentage of revenue? The pricing *model* matters as
  much as the sticker price.
- **How much implementation time do you have?** Be honest about this one.

## A word on implementation

Switching platforms is the part studios most underestimate. Onboarding,
data migration, staff training, and the "go-live" window vary a lot — from
free white-glove migrations on some platforms to do-it-yourself on others. We're
writing a dedicated piece on implementation types and realistic timelines; for
now, treat migration effort as a real line item in your decision, not an
afterthought.

## The landscape, in one line each

A neutral orientation (depth and opinion come in the
[feature comparison](/blog/studio-software-feature-comparison-2026)):

- **Mindbody** — the deep, enterprise incumbent, with a large consumer
  marketplace for discovery.
- **WellnessLiving** — the reporting-heavy "Mindbody alternative."
- **Walla** — modern boutique-studio favorite, clean UX, strong retention AI.
- **Arketa** — wellness-first challenger with a standout white-label app and
  video.
- **Momence** — video- and course-heavy all-in-one (now part of Xplor).
- **Vagaro** — broad, transparent generalist with real retail/POS.
- **Glofox** — app-first and franchise-minded (an ABC Fitness product).
- **Tandava** — our open-source, self-hosted project; honest about being an
  early-stage reference implementation, not a drop-in replacement for any of the
  above.
